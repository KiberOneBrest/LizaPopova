Currently, there are about 600 million domestic cats in the world [9], about 200 breeds have been bred, from long-haired (Persian cats) to hairless (Sphynx cats), recognized and registered by various felinological organizations. For 10,000 years, cats have been valued by humans, including their ability to hunt rodents and other household pests[10][11], as well as their ability to entertain and relieve stress.

The normal internal (rectal) body temperature of an adult cat is +38…+39.5 °C; in kittens it is slightly higher.
